## 1. Problem Statement
Consumers who want to avoid toxic or harmful ingredients in home and personal care products cannot act on that intent efficiently — product labels are confusing, "clean" marketing claims are unverified, and researching alternatives requires cross-referencing multiple fragmented sources. The research burden is high enough that most people give up and default to familiar, potentially harmful products.

## 2. ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
A consumer who has already decided they want cleaner home and personal care products but is stalled at the research phase. They are reading labels, Googling ingredients, cross-referencing EWG or similar databases — doing real investigative work — but finding it fragmented and time-consuming. They are accumulating mental lists of trusted brands and ingredients to avoid, but have no single place to store and apply that knowledge across repeated purchasing decisions.

## 3. Pain Points
- Product labels require domain expertise to interpret; most consumers don't have it
- "Clean," "natural," and "non-toxic" claims on packaging are unregulated and often misleading
- Research is fragmented across EWG, brand websites, review sites, and forums with no synthesis layer
- Preferences built up over time — trusted brands, avoided ingredients — have nowhere persistent to live
- Every new product purchase restarts the research process from scratch

## 4. Proposed Solution
Clean Shopper is a personal product research assistant with four core user actions:

1. **Search and research** — describe what you're looking for; receive AI-sourced product recommendations with ingredient safety assessments and reasoning drawn from EWG Skin Deep and real-time web search
2. **Save preferences** — store ingredients to avoid, trusted brands, and certifications that matter (EWG Verified, USDA Organic, B Corp); preferences are applied automatically to future recommendations
3. **Compare products** — ask for a side-by-side comparison of two or more products; receive a clear recommendation based on saved preferences
4. **Build a shopping cart** — add recommended products to a cart that persists across sessions
